# Multi-Validator Protocol for RQ2 Design Principles

## What this is

The six candidate design principles in `../RQ2_answer.md` were generated by a
verified RAG pipeline (see repository README). Each validator independently
reviews every principle against its supporting evidence and records a
decision (ACCEPT / REWORD / MERGE / REJECT) with written rationale. The
completed forms are published as the audit trail of human validation.

## Roles and files

| File | Validator |
|---|---|
| `validation_form_A.md` | Validator A (lead author) |
| `validation_form_B.md` | Validator B |
| `validation_form_C.md` | Validator C |

`.docx` copies of each form exist for validators who prefer Word; content is
identical. Each form is self-contained: it embeds the principles and their
supporting quotes verbatim, so a validator needs nothing else to start.
For deeper checking, the per-paper evidence (DOI, page, verbatim quote for
every coded item) is in `../paper_extractions.jsonl` in this repository.

## Rules

1. **Independence.** Complete your form without discussing decisions with
   other validators and without seeing their forms. Forms are reconciled only
   after all are submitted.
2. **One decision per principle**, with 1–3 sentences of rationale in your
   own words. Do not edit the "Pipeline output" blocks.
3. **Evidence-based.** Judge the principle against the quoted evidence (and
   the cited papers where needed), not against your prior expectations.
4. Date and sign the attestation.

## Reconciliation (after all forms are in)

1. Decisions are tabulated per principle (6 principles × N validators).
2. Inter-rater agreement is computed over the four-category decision
   (percent agreement; Cohen's kappa for 2 raters, Fleiss' kappa for 3+).
3. Disagreements are resolved by discussion to consensus; the consensus
   outcome and the agreement statistics are recorded in
   `validation_consensus.md` and applied to `../RQ2_answer.md` (original
   pipeline output preserved here as the audit trail).

## How to return your form

Any of:
- **GitHub (preferred, best audit trail):** fork
  `https://github.com/RMoHaNN/mlops-slr-rag`, fill in your form under
  `rag_results/validation/`, open a pull request.
- **Git, direct:** if you have write access, commit your completed form to a
  branch `validation/<your-initials>` and push.
- **Email:** return the completed `.md` or `.docx` to the lead author, who
  commits it verbatim on your behalf (the commit message records provenance).
